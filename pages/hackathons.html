Hackathons

Hackathons are collaborative events where participants work in teams to build projects that address a challenge within a short timeframe. A typical hackathon includes an orientation session, time for teams to form and brainstorm, an intensive coding phase, presentations of projects, and judging to determine winners【82189479256576†L46-L126】. Projects can range from apps and chatbots to games and hardware prototypes, and events are often themed around current technology trends【82189479256576†L42-L50】.

Many hackathons are beginner friendly: most events welcome anyone who registers and provide workshops to help newcomers get started【82189479256576†L55-L79】. They usually cost little to nothing to enter; in‑person hackathons typically provide meals so participants can focus on creating【82189479256576†L80-L85】.

High‑school hackathons have become especially popular. In 2025 there are hundreds of events across the world—Hack Club lists more than 700 upcoming high‑school hackathons in 30 states and 25 countries【47183827001825†L4-L8】. Elementary and middle‑school students have opportunities too; many competitions are open to ages 7–18 and encourage beginners to learn alongside more experienced coders.

Beyond STEMist Hacks, there are numerous hackathons each month for elementary, middle, high‑school and college students. Look for events hosted by universities, non‑profits and organizations like Hack Club or Devpost. Whether virtual or in‑person, these events are a great way to learn new skills, meet mentors and collaborators, and showcase your creativity.
